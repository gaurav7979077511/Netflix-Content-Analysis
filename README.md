## 📌 Project Overview

A comprehensive data analysis of Netflix's global content catalog (8,807 titles) to help the company improve its content strategy, understand audience preferences, and make better decisions for future production and regional expansion.

## 🎯 Business Problem

Netflix faces several challenges in content strategy:
- Which content performs best in different countries?
- How has content release trend changed over years?
- Should Netflix focus more on Movies or TV Shows?
- When is the best time to launch new content?
- Which genres attract the largest audience?

## 📊 Dataset

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Total Titles | 8,807 |
| Columns | 12 |
| Duplicates | 0 |
| Time Period | 1925 — 2021 |

## 🔍 Key Findings

### Content Distribution
- 🎬 Movies dominate — 6,131 (69.6%) vs TV Shows 2,676 (30.4%)
- 🇺🇸 US leads content production — 2,818 titles
- 🇮🇳 India focuses heavily on movies — 962 movies

### Ratings & Audience
- TV-MA is most common rating — 3,207 titles
- Mature content dominates Netflix catalog
- Family content comparatively lower

### Genre Insights
- International Movies — 2,752 titles
- Dramas — 2,427 titles
- Comedies — 1,674 titles
- International TV Shows growing rapidly

### Trends
- Content peaked around 2019
- COVID-19 reduced production in 2020
- TV Shows growing rapidly — may surpass movies

## 💡 Business Recommendations

1. **Invest More in International Content** — Global demand increasing
2. **Continue Mature Content Strategy** — TV-MA performs strongly
3. **Expand in India & UK** — High production potential
4. **Increase TV Show Investments** — Growing rapidly
5. **Explore Niche Genres** — Anime, Crime TV, Reality TV
6. **Improve Family Content Library** — Currently underrepresented
